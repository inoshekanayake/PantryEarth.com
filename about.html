How PantryEarth Started

Founded in 2024, PantryEarth was born out of a simple observations: too much premium organic fruit and spice goes to waste in local tropical farms due to sudden harvest overflows, while local markets get dry foods saturated with industrial chemical preservatives and heavy sugars.

We partnered with design engineers and local agricultural colleges to build secure, transparent solar-dehydration glass domes. These domes trap sunlight and circulate filtered hot air, drying our mangoes, cashews, spices, and mushrooms in a clean environment.

Today, we purchase directly from over 120 family-owned farms in Sri Lanka and Turkey. By paying fair wages directly, we help rural farmers sustain their lands while bringing raw, pure nutrition to your kitchen table.

Our Dehydration Process

Every order passes through four carefully designed steps to preserve original nutritional profiles.

1

Ethical Sourcing

Harvested fresh at peak sweetness and ripeness from certified pesticide-free organic partner orchards.

2

Ozone Wash

Washed inside spring-water tanks injected with natural ozone bubble purifiers, removing wild dirt safely.

3

Solar Dehydrate

Dried in closed glass domes using slow heat (45-55°C) to prevent moisture while preserving core natural enzymes.

4

Eco Packaging

Sealed in recycled brown paper pouches lined with plant-based liners. Plastic-free and earth friendly.

Sustainable Farming

Supporting Communities in Galle & Malatya

PantryEarth takes pride in our 100% fair trade commitment. Under-developed farming communities often face exploitation from large corporate brokers. We change this narrative.

👩‍🌾

Fair Wages & Work environments

We contractually secure wages that are 25% higher than government minimums, and co-fund farm medical insurance.

☀️

Solar Infrastructure Funding

We construct solar drying rooms on farmer lands. The farmers own these units, helping them dry other crops for local consumption.

🌳

Afforestation Pledge

For every 10kg of dry fruits or cinnamon quills sold, PantryEarth funds planting 1 native wild crop tree on local margins.
